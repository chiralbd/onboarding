# CHIRAL Recruitment Policy (Research Assistant & Group Leader)

CHIRAL recruits research members through a **prerequisite-then-interview** pathway. Eligibility
starts with successfully completing the **DeepBio Academy** course required for the target unit;
selection is by interview. This keeps a consistent baseline of skill and a clear, fair pipeline.

This policy is **center-wide**. The required course differs by unit (below). It complements the
[Center Guidelines → Appointment, Commitment & Affiliation](/shared/center-guidelines.md#appointment-commitment--affiliation).

---

## 1. Prerequisite Course by Unit

Eligibility requires **enrolling in and successfully completing** the relevant
[DeepBio Academy](/ECOSYSTEM.md) course:

| Unit | Required DeepBio Academy course |
|------|---------------------------------|
| **Big Bioinformatics Lab (BBL)** | Bioinformatics Mentorship |
| **BioHPC Lab** | Bioinformatics Mentorship |
| **Geospatial Health Research Group** | The Academic Research Workflow |
| **Population Health Studies Division** | The Academic Research Workflow |
| **BioAI Lab** | No Code and Agentic AI for Life Sciences |

"Successfully completed" means meeting the Academy's completion/certification criteria for that
course (see the [DeepBio Academy handbook](/ECOSYSTEM.md)).

---

## 2. Research Assistant (RA) Recruitment

1. **Enroll & complete** the required Academy course for the target unit.
2. **Apply** to the unit, providing proof of completion, and the disclosures required at
   onboarding (outside commitments — see Center Guidelines).
3. **Interview** with the unit's Group Leader (and the Director or a designate). Assesses skills,
   fit, motivation, and ability to meet the minimum-commitment expectation.
4. **Selection** by the Group Leader with the Director's approval.
5. **Onboarding:** sign the onboarding agreement, set up accounts, complete the mandatory
   affiliation/disclosure steps, and begin under a Group Leader.
6. **Probation:** the first **3 months** are a probationary period. Continuation depends on
   demonstrated contribution, reliability, and adherence to these guidelines.

Completing the course makes a candidate **eligible**, not automatically selected. Selection is
competitive and at CHIRAL's discretion.

---

## 3. Group Leader Recruitment

A Group Leader leads a unit/group and reports to the Director (see
[Roles & Reporting Structure](/shared/center-guidelines.md#roles--reporting-structure)).

- **Primary path — promotion:** an experienced RA with a strong track record (sustained
  contribution, authorship, reliability, mentorship of peers) is promoted by the Director.
- **Direct entry:** a candidate may enter as Group Leader if they have equivalent research
  experience and leadership ability. They must still have completed (or complete) the unit's
  required Academy course, and pass an interview with the **Director**.
- **Selection** is made by the **Director**.
- Group Leaders are held to the same commitment, affiliation, disclosure, and exclusivity rules
  as all members, plus the leadership responsibilities in the Center Guidelines.

---

## 4. Records & Fairness

- The required course, proof of completion, interview outcome, and selection decision are
  **recorded** for each recruit.
- Recruitment follows the [Code of Conduct](/shared/center-guidelines.md) — selection is on
  merit and fit, free of discrimination or harassment.
