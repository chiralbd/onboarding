Meetings

Rhythm of the week

Lab meeting — every Thursday, 9:30–11:00 — mandatory. Can't attend? Notify #meeting ≥ 24 h ahead with a reason.

  • Scrum: Friday kick-off (8:30) · Saturday demo day (8:30, 3-min wins) · daily Zulip update.
  • Research meeting: 1 hour, third Friday monthly.
  • Lead ≥ 2 meetings / semester (≥ 1 Braintrust). Sign up December / June.
  • Formats: Braintrust · Tech Talk · Post-Conference · Big Ideas · Journal Club · Preprint Review.
Mandatory

Affiliation & promotion

  • List your affiliation on LinkedIn and Facebook: “[Position], [Unit], CHIRAL, Bangladesh.”
  • Follow CHIRAL pages and share training & research activities.
  • Comply within 2 weeks of joining; keep it current.
  • No account? Create a professional one or get a Director exception — absence isn't an automatic exemption.
Disclosure

Exclusivity & disclosure

  • No concurrent membership in another research org/lab without the Director's prior written approval.
  • Disclose all outside commitments — at onboarding and within 7 days of any new one.
  • Your academic thesis (own degree) is exempt from approval — but still disclose it.
  • Failure to report is itself punishable. This is disclosure-and-approval, not a non-compete.
Section 04

Research Integrity

Code, data, authorship — done reproducibly and credited fairly.

Source Code

GitHub workflow

  • One central org: github.com/chiralbd. Public-by-default; private only when data/sponsor requires.
  • Sign your code — attributable to your GitHub account.
  • Default license: BSD-2-Clause Plus Patent.

Pull-request flow

Org repo → fork → commit → pull request → review. At least one approval before merge. PRs focus on a single functional area.

Reproducibility

Good practices

  • Never edit data via a GUI — write code, so it's traceable and repeatable.
  • Self-contained projects; no hardcoded setwd() paths.
  • Pinned environments: renv (R) / conda·uv + lockfile (Python).
  • Write code for people: clear names, real comments, consistent style.
  • Version-control every analysis; push daily; informative commits. Keep a lab book.
Health Data

Data governance

Public

Open / published data. Version-control the scripts.

Internal

De-identified study data. CHIRAL members only.

Sensitive

Identifiable / raw clinical / GPS. Strictest rules.

  • No identifiable human data without ethics/IRB approval and consent. When unsure → treat as Sensitive.
  • Encrypted, access-controlled storage only. Never commit data, keys, or credentials to Git.
  • Report breaches to the Director the same day.
Authorship

Publication & authorship

ICMJE — all four required

  • Substantial contribution
  • Draft or revise critically
  • Approve the final version
  • Accountable for the work

Order & credit

First = most work · last = senior/PI · middle = by contribution. No gift, guest, or ghost authorship. Record roles in a CRediT table from day one. Every author needs an ORCID.

Revisions

Revisions are part of authorship

  • Corresponding author circulates reviewer comments in 3 working days and assigns them.
  • Every author contributes to assigned comments by the internal deadline (≥ 5 working days before the journal deadline).
  • Silence ≠ approval. No contribution to a substantial revision → authorship may move down or to Acknowledgments.
  • Picking up abandoned revision work can move you up in order.
Accountability

The penalty ladder

Lapses (rolling 6 mo)Consequence
1stLogged verbal reminder + catch up
2ndWritten warning + catch-up plan; mentor informed
3rdAuthorship review (downgrade / Acknowledgments)
Persistent / severeRemoval from project + referral to Director

Applies to missed revisions, unexcused missed meetings, and missed communication windows. Penalties hit only the lapsing member — never reduce others' credit.

Ethics

Misconduct & ethics

  • Prohibited: gift/ghost authorship, plagiarism, data fabrication/falsification, undisclosed duplicate submission.
  • Be honest; minimize bias; keep agreements; share openly; give credit.
  • Maintain a safe, respectful, harassment-free environment — zero tolerance.
  • Consequences escalate to removal and revoked affiliation; illegal acts → authorities.
